<p><p>PVB resins, or polyvinyl butyral resins, are categorized into two market types based on viscosity: high viscosity and low viscosity. High viscosity PVB resins are commonly used in applications that require greater flexibility and impact resistance, such as in automotive safety glass. Low viscosity PVB resins, on the other hand, are preferred for easier processing and faster adhesion, making them suitable for applications like laminating glass and coatings. Both types serve distinct needs in various industries, influencing their demand and usage.</p></p>
