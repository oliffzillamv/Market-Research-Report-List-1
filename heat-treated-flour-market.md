<p><p>The heat-treated flour market is categorized into dry and wet segments. Dry heat-treated flour undergoes a high-temperature process that enhances shelf life and reduces microbial activity while retaining nutrients, making it suitable for baking and food processing. Conversely, wet heat-treated flour involves steaming or moist heat application, which alters the starch structure and improves hydration properties, ideal for specific recipes like noodles and steamed buns. Both types cater to diverse culinary needs, ensuring quality and safety in food production.</p></p>
