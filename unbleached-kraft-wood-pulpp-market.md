<p><p>The unbleached kraft wood pulp market is primarily divided into two segments: softwood pulp and hardwood pulp. Softwood pulp, derived from coniferous trees, is valued for its long fibers that contribute strength and durability, making it ideal for packaging and high-quality paper products. In contrast, hardwood pulp comes from deciduous trees and features shorter fibers, resulting in a smoother texture, which is preferred for printing papers and tissue products. Both types cater to different end-use applications in the paper industry.</p></p>
