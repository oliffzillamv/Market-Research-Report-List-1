<p><p>The Interstitial Free (IF) Sheet Steel market primarily consists of two types: Hot Rolled and Cold Rolled steels. Hot Rolled IF sheet steel is produced by rolling at high temperatures, resulting in a product that is generally less expensive and exhibits better ductility. In contrast, Cold Rolled IF sheet steel is processed at room temperature, offering improved surface finish, tighter tolerances, and enhanced strength. Both types cater to various applications in automotive and manufacturing industries, emphasizing formability and weldability.</p></p>
